<p><p>The galvanized reinforcing steel market consists of two primary types: conventional hot-dip galvanized rebar and continuous hot-dip galvanized rebar. Conventional hot-dip galvanized rebar is coated with zinc through a dip process, enhancing corrosion resistance for various construction applications. In contrast, continuous hot-dip galvanized rebar is produced through a continuous line process, ensuring uniform coating and greater efficiency in production. Both types serve to improve the longevity and durability of concrete structures, especially in harsh environments.</p></p>
