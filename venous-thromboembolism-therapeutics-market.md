<p><p>The Venous Thromboembolism (VTE) therapeutics market comprises various drug classes. Factor Xa inhibitors, like rivaroxaban and apixaban, prevent clot formation by inhibiting a key clotting enzyme. Direct thrombin inhibitors, such as dabigatran, work by directly blocking thrombin's action. Heparin, an anticoagulant, prevents clot extension and formation. Vitamin K antagonists, like warfarin, inhibit vitamin K-dependent clotting factors. The "Other" category includes emerging therapies and drugs that don't fall into these primary classes, expanding treatment options for VTE.</p></p>
