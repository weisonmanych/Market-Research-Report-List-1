<p><p>The external defibrillator market is primarily divided into two types: Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) and Manual External Defibrillators. AEDs are user-friendly devices designed for public use, providing voice prompts and visual instructions for laypersons to deliver lifesaving shocks in cardiac emergencies. Manual external defibrillators, on the other hand, are used by trained healthcare professionals and require operator decision-making for shock delivery, allowing for more controlled and precise interventions in emergency settings. Both types play crucial roles in cardiac care.</p></p>
