<p><p>The prescription arthritis medications market is categorized into oral and topical types. Oral medications, such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), are taken by mouth to reduce inflammation and manage pain systemically. Topical medications, including creams and gels, are applied directly to the skin over affected joints, providing localized relief with fewer systemic side effects. Both types aim to improve mobility and quality of life for arthritis patients through targeted therapeutic effects.</p></p>
