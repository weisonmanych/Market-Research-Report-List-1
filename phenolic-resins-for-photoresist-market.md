<p><p>Phenolic resins for photoresist applications primarily come in two forms: flake and block. Flake phenolic resins are finely divided particles that offer high surface area, enhancing their interaction with photoactive compounds and improving film uniformity. Block phenolic resins, on the other hand, are solid forms that allow for more straightforward handling and processing. Both types serve crucial roles in photolithography, influencing properties like resolution, contrast, and thermal stability, making them vital in semiconductor manufacturing and other advanced technologies.</p></p>
